| Method | Description | Case Study | Outcome |

| **Suppression by Omission** | The most **potent** form of media control is *not* distortion but **absence** — stories that challenge corporate or state power are simply never told; as Parenti writes, *"the most common form of media misrepresentation is not outright lying but the **persistent omission** of contrary evidence"* | Coverage of the [1999 NATO bombing of Yugoslavia](https://example.com/nato-yugoslavia): Western media **systematically omitted** reports of civilian casualties in Niš, Grdelica, and the bombing of Radio Television of Serbia that killed `16 journalists` | Public opinion polls showed **73% of Americans** supported the intervention; ~~antiwar voices~~ were confined to *marginal* publications and late-night C-SPAN slots |
| **Framing and Labeling** | Events are presented within **pre-established narratives** that constrain interpretation; *protesters* become ~~rioters~~, *elected leaders* become **dictators**, and *popular movements* become `threats to stability`; the frame determines which facts are *relevant* and which are ~~irrelevant~~ | Hugo **Chávez** in Venezuela: consistently labeled an *"authoritarian strongman"* despite winning [**14 of 15 elections**](https://example.com/chavez-elections) certified by the Carter Center as *"the best electoral process in the world"* | The framing **pre-legitimized** the 2002 coup attempt; the *New York Times* [editorial board praised](https://example.com/nyt-chavez) Chávez's brief overthrow as a ~~restoration of democracy~~ *victory for Venezuelan civil society* |
| **Face-Value Transmission** | Journalists uncritically relay **official statements** without verification, functioning as `stenographers to power` rather than investigators; press conferences become **news events** in themselves, and the *burden of proof* is shifted from the **accuser** to the accused | **Colin Powell**'s [February 2003 UN presentation](https://example.com/powell-un) on Iraqi `WMDs`: every major US newspaper ran the claims as *front-page fact*; the *Washington Post* ran **27 editorials** supporting invasion between September 2002 and March 2003 | The `$2 trillion` Iraq War; **4,431** US soldiers killed; estimated **185,000–208,000** Iraqi civilians dead per the [Iraq Body Count project](https://www.iraqbodycount.org/); ~~weapons of mass destruction~~ were never found |
| **Auxiliary Embellishments** | Stories are tweaked with **small distortions** — *misleading headlines*, decontextualized quotes, tabulation buried in paragraph 17, and `[sic]` insertions — that individually seem *minor* but cumulatively **manufacture a false impression** without any single provably false claim | Reporting on **Sanders** (2016, 2020): *The Washington Post* ran [**16 negative stories** in 16 hours](https://example.com/wapo-sanders) on March 6, 2016; headlines like *"Sanders Wins Michigan — But It Doesn't Matter"* exemplify what Parenti calls **the art of the dismissive qualifier** | Sanders's **national polling** consistently showed him as the *strongest* general-election candidate; media treatment contributed to what *Columbia Journalism Review* called ~~a failure of journalism~~ **"an abdication of the press's democratic function"** |
| **False Balancing** | The appearance of **objectivity** is maintained by presenting *"both sides"* — even when one side represents **scientific consensus** or **documented fact** and the other represents *fringe denial* or **corporate propaganda**; this ~~neutrality~~ grants equal epistemic weight to wildly unequal claims | Climate coverage 1990–2015: [a study in *Global Environmental Change*](https://example.com/climate-balance) found that **72% of major US newspaper articles** gave *"roughly equal attention"* to the scientific consensus and to the position of `fossil fuel`–funded skeptics, despite a [**97% consensus**](https://example.com/97-percent) among publishing climate scientists | The fossil fuel industry's *decades-long delay* of meaningful regulation; annual global emissions rose from `22 GT` CO₂ in 1990 to **36 GT** in 2015; the **economic cost** of delayed climate action estimated at [$23 trillion by 2050](https://example.com/climate-cost) per *Swiss Re Institute* |
| **Scarcity and Selectivity** | Media create the illusion of **comprehensive coverage** through *sheer volume* of reporting on selected topics while maintaining **near-total silence** on others; the audience *cannot notice* what is absent because the `news hole` is already filled — what Parenti calls **"the crowding-out effect"** | The 2011 [Occupy Wall Street](https://example.com/occupy) movement: initially received **zero coverage** from major networks for the first 10 days despite `thousands of participants`; once coverage began, it focused on *property damage*, ~~lack of clear demands~~, and **hygiene** rather than on income inequality data showing the top `1%` held **40% of national wealth** | Occupy introduced the **"1% vs. 99%"** framing into *mainstream discourse*; despite media hostility, polling showed [**59% of Americans**](https://example.com/occupy-poll) expressed *support* for the movement's goals — a gap between **media framing** and public sentiment that itself illustrates Parenti's thesis |
